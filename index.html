Master's Research

Electron transport and semiconductor reliability

Monte Carlo Modeling of Electron-Electron Scattering and Cryogenic Hot-Carrier Reliability

Arizona State University · M.S. Electrical Engineering · 2026

This research examines how microscopic carrier-scattering and thermal processes shape the high-energy electron distribution relevant to semiconductor reliability. The work combines a MATLAB bulk Monte Carlo implementation of electron-electron and phonon scattering with analysis of self-heating and cryogenic transport behavior.

  • Two-particle electron-electron scattering in ensemble Monte Carlo
  • Electron distribution functions and high-energy carrier populations
  • Phonon scattering, self-heating, and energy exchange
  • Cryogenic operation at 300 K, 77 K, and 4.2 K
